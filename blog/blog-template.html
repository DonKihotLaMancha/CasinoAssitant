2. Taking Insurance

Insurance is a sucker bet with a house edge of 7.5%. Unless you're counting cards and know the deck is rich in tens, always decline insurance.

3. Playing 6:5 Blackjack

Traditional blackjack pays 3:2 for a natural 21. Avoid tables that pay 6:5 - this increases the house edge by 1.4%!

4. Poor Bankroll Management

Never bet more than 1-2% of your total bankroll per hand. This ensures you can weather losing streaks.

5. Splitting 10s

You already have 20 - one of the best hands in blackjack. Never split tens, even if the dealer shows a weak card.

6. Not Splitting Aces and 8s

Always split Aces and 8s. Two hands starting with an Ace give you great winning potential, and splitting 8s turns a bad 16 into two chances at 18.

7. Chasing Losses

Increasing bets after losses (Martingale system) is dangerous. Stick to your betting unit regardless of recent results.

8. Playing When Tired or Drunk

Casinos offer free drinks for a reason. Alcohol impairs judgment and leads to costly mistakes.

9. Ignoring Table Rules

Different tables have different rules. Look for:

  • Dealer stands on soft 17 (better for player)
  • Double after split allowed
  • Surrender option available
  • Fewer decks (single deck is best)
